About me

Hi, I’m Nicolás Tanzi, an economist and data scientist based in Buenos Aires, Argentina. I hold a master's in economics ,and currently, I’m pursuing a Master’s in Data Science online from the University of Texas at Austin.

With a background in macroeconomic consulting and expertise in econometrics, time series analysis, machine learning, and deep learning, I’ve applied my skills to projects ranging from financial modeling to large language model applications. Education

I hold a bachelor's degree in economics from the University of Buenos Aires and a master's in economics from the University of San Andrés. Currently, I’m pursuing a Master’s in Data Science online from the University of Texas at Austin.

Current Position

I am Model Validator at KDOA. I scrutinize models with great rigor, perform backtesting to evaluate their accuracy, and compare them against benchmark models that I develop. Additionally, I conduct sensitivity analysis to stress-test the model and observe how it responds. Finally, every validation ends with a document describing the model, presenting the analysis, and raising the issues found, which envolves report writing, smoothness to comunicate the findings as well as openess to hear the model owner's feed back.

Coding Skills

Coding has always been a lot of fun for me. When I was fifteen, I taught myself HTML and CSS. Initially, I created simple web pages to play pranks on my friends, but then I helped my father create his own website.

While studying economics, I became devoted to R (great for econometrics) and Julia (similar to MATLAB and excellent for complex macroeconomic models). Later, I developed an interest in Python and Data Science, which has now become the main language I use.

Additionally, I have experience with SAS and SQL, which I used to develop an ALM model for Banco Hipotecario, and LaTeX, which I mastered while working as a research assistant, particularly excelling with my TikZ plots.
