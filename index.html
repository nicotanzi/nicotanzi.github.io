A Brief Introduction

Hi, I’m Horacio Nicolás Tanzi, but you can call me Nico. I'm an economist and data scientist based in Buenos Aires, Argentina. I hold a master's in economics and am currently pursuing an online Master’s in Data Science from the University of Texas at Austin.

With a background in macroeconomic consulting and expertise in econometrics, time series analysis, machine learning, and deep learning, I’ve applied my skills to projects ranging from economic forecasting to machine learning financial models. I’m passionate about solving complex problems by combining economics, data, and technology.

Key Highlights

Current Position

I am a Model Validator at KDOA. I scrutinize models with great rigor, perform backtesting to evaluate their accuracy, and compare them against benchmark models I develop. Additionally, I conduct sensitivity analysis to stress-test the model and observe how it responds. Finally, every validation ends with a document describing the model, presenting the analysis, and raising the issues found, which involves report writing, smoothness to communicate the findings as well as openness to hear the model owner's feedback.

Academic Background

I hold a bachelor's degree in economics from the University of Buenos Aires, where I also served as a Teaching Assistant for Microeconomics I, Microeconomics II, and Statistics II. I earned a master's degree in economics from the University of San Andrés, where my Recursive Macroeconomics professor took me as his research assistant. Currently, I am pursuing a Master’s in Data Science online from the University of Texas at Austin, having successfully completed 5 out of 10 subjects, including Regressions, Data Structures & Algorithms, Statistics & Probability, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. These courses have required extensive Python coding, and I am on track to complete the program in 2025.

Coding Skills

Coding has always been a passion of mine. I taught myself HTML and CSS at fifteen, creating simple web pages.
